Downy Mildew in Rose Plants
Causes:
	Rose downy mildew disease is caused by Peronospora sparsa, an obligate biotrophic oomycete. 
	This disease is one of the most destructive diseases of rose plants and attacks all types of roses in greenhouse, nursery and landscape settings.
Treatment:
	Increase air circulation and reduce excess moisture by thinning canes and reducing foliage where possible. 
	Proper spacing will also improve air circulation and help prevent spread of the disease.
